Module II

Presence & Sound

Four weeks of depth, practice, and presence — building a track that is entirely yours.

Where you begin

You have the foundation from Module I. You understand your tools, you can shape sound. Now you are ready to go deeper — to cultivate a real connection to sound through awareness and presence.

The techniques are there. What is needed now is depth, practice, and the patience to let something whole emerge.

Where this leads

A completed track — designed as a reference piece for your musical journey. You will use the techniques and tools from Module I to craft your unique sound, working from embodied presence rather than reactive habit.

By the end, you have not just learned — you have created something that is entirely yours.

What happens inside

Week 1

Arriving

Grounding yourself in the present moment, creating a strong foundation for the creative work ahead. You leave with a grounding ritual you can repeat before every production session, and a new Logic Pro session template set up to your workflow.

Week 2

Listening

Active listening before shaping sounds. You build a personal listening log — an annotated report on 1–3 reference tracks broken down sonically. Understanding how atmosphere, space, and emotion are constructed in your favourite music or your own work.

Week 3

Body

Exploring how movement and physicality influence your creative expression. You build a completed sound palette — a folder of original sounds, samples, and textures you have designed yourself, alongside samples I share with you. First draft of a track arrangement built around those sounds.

Week 4

Integration

Blending everything into a completed reference track — a full composition that represents your sound world. You receive a feedback recording from Eric reviewing the track with timestamp notes, and a progress reflection document summarising your journey, breakthroughs, and next chapter.

What you walk away with
  • A grounding ritual or pre-session practice you can repeat before every production session
  • A Logic Pro session template set up to your workflow
  • A personal listening log — annotated sonic breakdown of reference tracks
  • A completed sound palette — original sounds, samples, and textures you have designed
  • A completed reference track — a full composition that represents your sound world
  • A feedback recording from Eric reviewing your track with timestamp notes
  • A progress reflection document summarising your journey, breakthroughs, and next chapter
  • Direct access to Eric via phone and WhatsApp for async support throughout
After this module you will
  • Have a reference track and template you can use for all your projects
  • Understand your harmonic language
  • Develop a personal sonic identity
  • Design a complete sound palette to create EPs and albums
  • Get constant feedback from Eric whenever needed
